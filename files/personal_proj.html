Amateur Rocketry

Higher, Faster, Further

Building rockets to fly in the California desert is a very fulfilling hobby. Construction allows me to hone my mechanical skills, and I get to be creative when painting.

Of course the ultimate payoff is lighting the fire and watching it soar. This video shows my Level 1 Certification launch. I built the rocket from a Madcow Torrent kit and dubbed it "Charlie Horse." It flew on a Cesaroni 225I Motor and reached roughly 3,000 ft altitude.

The rocket was recovered without damage and is being prepped for a Level 2 Certification flight early next year, which will include a flight computer and dual chute deployment. I hope to design and build my next high power rocket from scratch.

Volunteering at Tomorrow's Aeronautical Museum

Rocketry Program Coordinator

I go to the Compton airport at least once a week to teach kids ages 8 - 16 about the basics of rocketry and space travel. Topics range from Newton's Laws to the makeup of our Solar System and any other technical topic the students have questions about.

The lectures and discussions are balanced out with hands-on demonstrations of the principals covered. These demos include alka-seltzer "rockets", building and flying Estes model rockets, and use of the Kerbal Space Program video game to inspire the kids and make the topics more accessable.
